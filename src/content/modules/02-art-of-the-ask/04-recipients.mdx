## Who is this for?

The **R** in PARTS stands for **Recipients** — the people who will actually use or receive what AI creates for you.

This is one of the most powerful elements of the PARTS framework, because it controls:
- The language level and complexity of the output
- The cultural references and examples used
- The appropriateness of the tone
- The relevance of the content to your specific learners

---

## What to include in Recipients

**Grade and age:**
"Grade 8 learners aged 13-14" tells AI a lot about cognitive development, vocabulary level, and what kinds of examples will resonate.

**Language context:**
South Africa is a multilingual country. Telling AI that your learners' home language is isiZulu, Afrikaans, or Sesotho — and that they are learning in English as a first additional language — dramatically changes the appropriate vocabulary level and sentence structure.

**Learning needs:**
If you are creating content for learners who need additional support, include that. If you are creating content for learners with IEPs, gifted learners, or learners with reading difficulties, say so.

**Cultural context:**
Your learners in Soweto have different reference points than learners in Stellenbosch or Durban. Including cultural context helps AI choose relevant examples.

---

## Recipients in the South African classroom

This element is particularly important for SA educators because of our country's remarkable diversity. A Grade 9 class might include:

- Learners from multiple home language backgrounds
- Learners in both urban and rural settings
- Learners with access to very different home resources
- Learners who relate to a wide variety of cultural references

The more specific you are about your Recipients, the more appropriate the output will be.

<SAExample title="How Recipients change the output">
**Same Aim, different Recipients:**

**Recipients:** "Grade 10 learners in a well-resourced private school in Cape Town."
*AI produces:* Activities with references to international examples, complex vocabulary, independent research tasks.

**Recipients:** "Grade 10 learners at a township school in the East Rand. English is a first additional language for most learners. The school has limited textbooks and no computers."
*AI produces:* Activities using simpler vocabulary, local examples, tasks that do not require internet access, and more scaffolding.

Both requests are valid. The difference is that the second one produces something you can actually use in your specific classroom.
</SAExample>

<TryItNow title="Write Recipients for your class">
Think about one of your current classes. Write a Recipients description that includes:

1. Grade and approximate age
2. Home language(s) most common in your class
3. Any notable learning needs, strengths, or contexts
4. One cultural reference point that is relevant to your learners

This becomes a reusable part of your prompts whenever you are creating content for that class.
