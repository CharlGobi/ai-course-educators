## The intern analogy

Imagine you have just hired a brilliant new intern. They are incredibly fast, they can write beautifully, and they have read more books than any person alive. But they have never been to your school. They do not know your learners. They do not know anything about your context.

On their first day, you say: *"Can you make me a quiz?"*

They stare at you. A quiz about what? For who? How long? Multiple choice or short answer? Easy or difficult?

They do their best and produce something generic. It is not quite what you needed.

Now imagine instead you say: *"Can you create a 10-question multiple choice quiz on ecosystems for my Grade 7 Natural Sciences class? The learners are 12-13 years old and this is the end of Term 2. Include four options per question and a brief explanation for each correct answer."*

That intern now produces something genuinely useful.

**Gemini is that intern.** The quality of what it gives you is almost entirely determined by the quality of what you ask.

## You are the director

The single most helpful mindset shift is this: **you are not asking AI a question. You are directing a very capable assistant.**

A film director does not walk onto set and say "do something interesting." They give specific instructions: who is in the scene, what they are doing, the tone, the setting, and the outcome.

When you give Gemini a specific, detailed brief, it responds with specific, useful output. When you give it a vague brief, it does its best — but "its best" with limited information is rarely what you actually needed.

The good news: you do not need to be technical. You do not need to learn code. You just need to give clear instructions in plain language. And there is a simple framework that makes this easy.

That framework is called **PARTS** — and you will learn it in the next four lessons.

<TryItNow title="Improve a vague prompt">
Look at this vague prompt:

> *"Create something for parents about the school year."*

On paper or in a text file, rewrite this prompt to be as specific as possible. Think about:
- Who are you?
- What exactly do you want AI to create?
- Who will receive it?
- What should the tone or style be?
- How should it be formatted?

There is no right answer here. Just practise the mindset of giving more detail.
</TryItNow>
