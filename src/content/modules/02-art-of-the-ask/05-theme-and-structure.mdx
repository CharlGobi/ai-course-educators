## The final two ingredients

You have learned P (Persona), A (Aim), and R (Recipients). Now let us complete the PARTS framework with the final two elements: **Theme** and **Structure**.

Together, these five elements give AI everything it needs to produce output that is ready to use — or very close to it.

---

## T is for Theme

**Theme** is the subject matter, context, and any special parameters your request needs.

This is where you add:
- The topic or content area
- South African cultural context
- Curriculum-specific requirements (e.g. CAPS)
- Any constraints or special considerations

Think of Theme as the "what it is about" — going beyond just stating the aim.

**Example Theme additions:**
- "...covering the key content from the Grade 8 CAPS curriculum for Term 3"
- "...using examples relevant to South African learners: local food, sport, geography"
- "...appropriate for a multilingual classroom where English is a first additional language for most learners"
- "...themed around Heritage Day and the diversity of South African culture"

---

## S is for Structure

**Structure** tells AI how you want the output formatted.

Without this, AI will choose a format for you — and it might not match what you need. With Structure, you get output that is ready to copy, print, or send.

**Common Structure instructions:**
- "Format as a lesson plan with sections for: objectives, introduction, main activity, and closure."
- "Present as a table with two columns: Afrikaans term on the left, English definition on the right."
- "Write as five bullet points, each no longer than two sentences."
- "Format as a multiple choice quiz with four options per question."
- "Present as a friendly, informal newsletter of no more than 200 words."
- "Create as a rubric with four levels of achievement, listed from highest to lowest."

<KeyPoint title="Structure saves you editing time" variant="tip">
The more specific your Structure instruction, the less editing you need to do after Gemini responds. Telling AI to write a 200-word parent letter in a friendly tone means you get something close to ready — rather than a 600-word formal document you have to cut down.
</KeyPoint>

## Putting it all together: A complete PARTS prompt

Here is a full worked example using all five elements for a Grade 10 IsiZulu teacher:

<PromptBox
  title="Complete PARTS prompt — worked example"
  example="I am an experienced Grade 10 IsiZulu Home Language teacher at a public high school in KwaZulu-Natal. (PERSONA)

Please create a vocabulary matching activity. (AIM)

This is for Grade 10 learners whose home language is IsiZulu and who are preparing for their end-of-term assessment. (RECIPIENTS)

The activity should focus on commonly misused vocabulary words from the prescribed novel 'Ukufa KukaShaka', with a focus on figurative language terms. (THEME)

Format the activity as a two-column table: the IsiZulu term in the left column, and the definition in the right column, with 15 items. Also include an answer key on a separate page. (STRUCTURE)"
/>

Notice how much context that gives Gemini. The result will be something you can almost use directly — perhaps with a few small tweaks.
