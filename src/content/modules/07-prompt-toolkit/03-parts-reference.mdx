## The PARTS framework at a glance

You spent all of Module 2 learning the PARTS framework. This lesson is your quick-reference card — the condensed version you return to when you are writing a prompt and want to make sure you have covered all five elements.

---

## PARTS: the five elements

| Letter | Element | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P** | Persona | Tells AI what role to play and how to communicate | *"You are a patient, friendly Grade 4 teacher..."* |
| **A** | Aim | States the precise purpose of the prompt | *"Create a lesson plan / Write a letter / Explain a concept"* |
| **R** | Recipients | Describes who will receive or use the output | *"...for Grade 8 learners whose home language is IsiZulu"* |
| **T** | Theme | States the specific topic or content | *"...about the water cycle in the context of South African rainfall patterns"* |
| **S** | Structure | Tells AI how to format or organise the response | *"...as a numbered list / in a table / in three short paragraphs"* |

---

## A complete PARTS prompt example

Here is how all five elements combine in a real prompt:

> **[P]** You are an experienced Grade 10 Life Sciences teacher in South Africa.
> **[A]** Create a structured study guide
> **[R]** for Grade 10 learners preparing for their mid-year examination.
> **[T]** The guide should cover the topic of evolution: natural selection, variation, adaptation, and the fossil record. All content must align with CAPS.
> **[S]** Format it as a one-page study guide with: key concepts as bullet points, definitions in bold, and three practice questions at the end.

## Quick-reference: common Aim verbs

| Task type | Aim verb to use |
|---|---|
| Create a new document | Create, Draft, Generate, Write, Design |
| Improve something existing | Improve, Refine, Rewrite, Simplify, Expand |
| Explain something | Explain, Describe, Define, Summarise, Clarify |
| Analyse or review | Analyse, Compare, Evaluate, Assess, Review |
| Plan or organise | Plan, Outline, Organise, Sequence, Schedule |
| Translate or adapt | Translate, Adapt, Convert, Simplify, Reformat |

## Common Recipient phrases for SA educators

- *"...for Grade [X] learners in a South African public school"*
- *"...for learners whose home language is [language]"*
- *"...for learners reading 2 years below grade level"*
- *"...for parents and guardians, written in plain English"*
- *"...for a class of 45 learners with mixed ability levels"*
- *"...for a School Governing Body audience"*

---

## Common Structure phrases

- *"Format as a numbered step-by-step guide"*
- *"Present as a table with [columns]"*
- *"Keep it to one page"*
- *"Use bullet points throughout"*
- *"Include a short introduction, three main sections, and a conclusion"*
- *"Write at a Grade [X] reading level"*
- *"Maximum [X] words"*

## When PARTS is not enough

Sometimes AI still misses what you need, even with a full PARTS prompt. Here are three ways to improve the result:

**Add an example:**
> *"Here is an example of the style and format I am looking for: [paste example]"*

**Ask for alternatives:**
> *"Give me three different versions of this and I will choose the best one."*

**Iterate:**
> *"Good start. Now make it shorter, more informal, and replace the word 'students' with 'learners'."*

The best prompts are often the result of two or three exchanges, not one perfect attempt. Treat AI like a conversation, not a search engine.
