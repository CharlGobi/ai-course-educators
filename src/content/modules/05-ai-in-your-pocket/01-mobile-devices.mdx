## Your phone is a powerful AI terminal

Most South African teachers access the internet primarily through their mobile phone. The good news: Gemini works just as well on a phone as it does on a computer. In some ways, it is even easier to use on mobile.

This lesson walks you through using Gemini on your smartphone — whether you have an Android or iPhone — and introduces voice input, which removes the need to type at all.

---

## Setting up Gemini on Android

Google Gemini is built into Android phones running Android 10 or later. Here is how to access it:

**Option 1 — Google app:**
1. Open the Google app on your phone
2. Tap the Gemini icon (a sparkle symbol) in the search bar
3. Sign in with your Google account
4. Start chatting

**Option 2 — Dedicated app:**
1. Go to the Google Play Store
2. Search for "Google Gemini"
3. Install the app (it is free)
4. Sign in with your Google account

**Option 3 — Web browser:**
1. Open Chrome or any browser
2. Go to gemini.google.com
3. Sign in and start using Gemini directly in your browser

<KeyPoint type="tip">
If your phone is running an older version of Android or has limited storage, the web browser option (gemini.google.com) works just as well and requires no app installation.
</KeyPoint>

## Setting up Gemini on iPhone

1. Open the App Store
2. Search for "Google Gemini"
3. Install the app (it is free)
4. Sign in with your Google account

Alternatively, open Safari and go to gemini.google.com.

## Using voice input — no typing needed

Typing a long prompt on a phone keyboard takes time. Voice input solves this.

**In the Gemini app:**
1. Tap the microphone icon in the message box
2. Speak your prompt clearly
3. Gemini transcribes and responds

**In any browser:**
1. Tap the text box where you would type
2. Tap the microphone on your phone keyboard
3. Speak your prompt

<TryItNow>
Open Gemini on your phone right now. Tap the microphone and say:

*"I am a Grade 5 teacher in South Africa. Give me three fun starter questions I can ask my class to begin a lesson about fractions."*

Notice how fast this is compared to typing. Voice input is particularly useful when you are in your car, on a taxi, or moving between classrooms.
</TryItNow>

## Saving responses for offline use

AI requires an internet connection to generate responses. But once you have a response, you can save it for use without data.

**Three ways to save AI responses:**

1. **Copy and paste** — Select the text, copy it, paste it into your phone's Notes app or Google Keep
2. **Screenshot** — Take a screenshot of the response to view later without data
3. **Google Docs** — Open a shared Google Doc and paste important AI outputs there; they will be accessible offline if you have set Docs to offline mode

<SAExample title="Prompting on the taxi to work">
Mr Nkosi teaches at two schools and commutes for 90 minutes each day. He uses the time on the taxi to prepare using his phone.

"I do my voice prompts on the way to school in the morning. By the time I arrive, I have three or four ideas ready. I copy the best one into my notes, and I use it in first period. My colleagues think I am very organised. Really I am just using my travel time."

His tip: use earphones so you can speak softly without disturbing other passengers.
</SAExample>

## Data-saving tips for mobile AI use

- Gemini text conversations use very little data — roughly equivalent to loading a webpage
- Avoid uploading large images or documents when on mobile data (save this for Wi-Fi)
- Use Gemini's text interface rather than voice-to-voice (audio uses more data)
- Set your phone's Gemini app to use Wi-Fi only if you want to control data spend

## Keyboard shortcuts worth knowing

| Action | How to do it |
|---|---|
| Start a new chat | Tap the pencil/compose icon |
| Copy a response | Long-press on text to select, then copy |
| Share a response | Tap the share icon below any response |
| Clear chat history | In settings, select "Clear conversation" |
| Use voice input | Tap microphone icon in the text box |
