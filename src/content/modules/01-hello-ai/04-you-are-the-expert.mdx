## The most important thing to remember

Before you go any further in this course, there is one idea you should hold onto throughout:

**You are the expert. AI is the assistant.**

This is not just a reassuring statement. It is technically true. AI does not know your learners. It does not know your school. It does not understand the context of a classroom in Alexandra, or Stellenbosch, or Musina, or any other South African community. It cannot walk into your classroom and read the room.

You can.

---

## What AI cannot do

Let us be specific about the limitations that matter most for South African teachers:

**AI does not know your individual learners.**
It does not know that Thandi has been absent for three weeks because of a family crisis. It does not know that your class is energised after sports day, or exhausted before the holidays. You bring this human intelligence to every lesson.

**AI does not understand your community.**
The examples and analogies that resonate with your Grade 9s in Khayelitsha are different from what would resonate with learners in Pretoria or Durban. You know what cultural references will land, what language will connect, and what topics are sensitive. AI needs you to guide it.

**AI does not share your professional ethics.**
You are bound by the South African Council of Educators (SACE) code of ethics. You are a trusted professional in your community. AI is a tool. The responsibility for what happens in your classroom always sits with you.

**AI does not replace your judgement.**
When AI generates a lesson plan or an assessment, it is a starting point. You decide whether it is appropriate for your class, whether it is CAPS-aligned, whether the language level is right, and whether the content is accurate.

---

## Think of AI as a junior colleague

The most useful frame for working with AI is this: imagine you have been given a very bright, very fast, never-tired **junior colleague**.

This colleague:
- Can write a first draft of almost anything in seconds
- Has read more textbooks and teaching resources than any human possibly could
- Never complains about repetitive tasks
- Is available at midnight when you are finally finishing your prep
- Does not know your learners, your school, or your community
- Can be confidently wrong about specific facts
- Needs your guidance and review before anything goes in front of learners

You would not hand a junior colleague's work directly to your learners without reviewing it. The same principle applies to AI. Check it. Edit it. Make it yours.

<SAExample title="AI as your prep assistant">
Here is how Ms Nkosi, a Grade 4 teacher in Johannesburg, describes using AI:

*"I used to spend Sunday afternoons writing newsletters and creating worksheets. Now I type what I need into Gemini, read through the result, change a few things to fit my class, and I am done in 20 minutes instead of two hours. I still write everything that goes to parents and learners — AI just gives me a very good first draft."*

This is the realistic, empowering use of AI. Not replacing the teacher. Making the teacher's Sunday afternoons a little less stressful.
</SAExample>

## Your commitment to this course

This course is self-paced. There is no test at the end that determines whether you are a "good" teacher. The only measure that matters is whether what you learn here makes your working life a little easier and your learners' experience a little richer.

Take it at whatever pace works for you. Come back to it when you have a spare moment. Skip forward to the modules that solve your most pressing problems first.

The tools you learn here will not expire. Gemini and NotebookLM will only get more capable over time, and the prompting skills you build in Module 2 will remain valuable regardless of which AI tools you use in the future.
