## A different kind of AI tool

While Gemini is a general-purpose AI assistant that draws on everything it has learned from across the internet, **NotebookLM** works differently — and that difference is very important for teachers.

NotebookLM is Google's **document-based AI**. It only uses the sources that **you** give it. Nothing else.

This means when you upload your Grade 8 History textbook chapter and ask NotebookLM questions about it, it answers using only that document. It will not mix in information from other sources. And crucially, it will **tell you exactly which part of your document it is drawing from**.

You can access NotebookLM for free at [notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com).

---

## Why this matters for South African educators

This is genuinely game-changing for lesson preparation. Here is why:

You can upload your CAPS documents, textbook chapters, past exam papers, or your own lesson notes — and then have a conversation with that material. Ask it to:

- Summarise the key topics for a term
- Generate comprehension questions from a reading passage
- Create a study guide from a chapter
- Identify the main themes in a document
- Find all references to a specific concept
- Generate flashcards from your source material

Everything NotebookLM tells you is **grounded in your documents**. It will not hallucinate information that is not in your sources. This makes it far more reliable for curriculum-specific work than general Gemini.

<KeyPoint title="Gemini vs NotebookLM" variant="info">
**Use Gemini when:** you want to create something new — a lesson plan, an activity, a newsletter, a quiz. It uses its broad knowledge.

**Use NotebookLM when:** you want to work with specific documents you already have — summarising, questioning, and generating content based only on those sources.
</KeyPoint>

## How to use NotebookLM

Using NotebookLM takes three steps:

**Step 1: Create a new notebook**
When you open NotebookLM, you will see a button to create a new notebook. Each notebook is a separate workspace. You might have one notebook for each grade or subject you teach.

**Step 2: Upload your sources**
You can add:
- PDF files (like CAPS documents, textbook chapters, past papers)
- Google Docs
- Text you paste in directly
- YouTube videos (by pasting the URL)
- Website URLs

**Step 3: Ask questions in the chat**
Once your sources are uploaded, type your question in the chat on the right side. NotebookLM will answer based only on your documents, and will show you **citations** — references to the exact part of your document where the information comes from.

---

## What NotebookLM can generate for you

Beyond answering questions, NotebookLM can automatically generate:

- **Study guides** — a structured summary of all the key concepts in your documents
- **Quiz questions** — with answers, based on your source material
- **Mind maps** — a visual overview of how concepts connect
- **Audio overviews** — a spoken podcast-style discussion of your content (great for revision!)
- **FAQ documents** — common questions and answers about your topic

These are found in the "Notebook guide" panel on the right when you open a notebook.

<SAExample title="Using NotebookLM for CAPS planning">
Imagine you are a Grade 10 Life Sciences teacher. You upload the Grade 10 Life Sciences CAPS document to a new NotebookLM notebook.

You can then ask:
- *"What are the key content areas for Term 3?"*
- *"List all the formal assessment tasks required in Grade 10."*
- *"What prior knowledge do learners need before studying biodiversity?"*
- *"Create 10 short-answer questions for the topic of photosynthesis."*

Every answer will be based on the actual CAPS document — not on what Gemini thinks the curriculum might be. This is far more reliable for assessment planning.
</SAExample>
