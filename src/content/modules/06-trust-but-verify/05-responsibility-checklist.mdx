## Your five-point check before every use

You have now covered everything in the Trust But Verify module: hallucinations, bias, POPIA, and academic integrity. This final lesson brings it all together in one practical tool.

The AI Responsibility Checklist is a five-point check you can run through in under two minutes, every time you plan to use AI-generated content in your classroom or communications.

Print it. Keep it near your desk. Use it until the questions become second nature.

---

## The Five-Point Responsibility Check

<Checklist items={[
  "1. ACCURACY: Have I checked the key facts, dates, and figures in this content for accuracy?",
  "2. BIAS: Have I read this for cultural assumptions, stereotypes, or contexts that do not fit my learners?",
  "3. PRIVACY: Did I avoid entering any learner personal information when generating this content?",
  "4. CAPS ALIGNMENT: Does this content match what my learners are expected to know at their grade level?",
  "5. MY VOICE: Have I edited this to reflect my professional judgment and the needs of my specific class?"
]} />

If you can tick all five, the content is ready to use.

If any item is unticked, address it before using the content.

---

## Why each check matters

**1. Accuracy**
AI confidently produces incorrect facts, invented statistics, and plausible-sounding errors. A single unchecked fact that turns out to be wrong can undermine your credibility and misinform your learners. Always verify anything you cannot personally confirm.

**2. Bias**
AI-generated examples often reflect the world AI was trained on — which is largely Western and American. Check for examples, names, and contexts that feel foreign to your learners. Substitute them for something locally relevant.

**3. Privacy**
POPIA requires you to protect personal information. The checklist reminds you that clean AI inputs lead to clean AI outputs — and no accidental data exposure.

**4. CAPS alignment**
AI does not know your Annual Teaching Plan (ATP) or what your class has already covered. Generated content may include material above or below your grade level, or content scheduled for a different term. Always verify curriculum fit.

**5. Your voice**
AI gives you a first draft. Your professional judgment, your knowledge of your learners, and your years of teaching experience are what make that draft into something genuinely useful. The final product should sound like you, not like a machine.

---

## Using the checklist in practice

The checklist does not need to slow you down. Once you know the five points, running through them takes about 90 seconds.

**Quick scan approach:**
Read through the AI-generated content once while mentally running the five checks. Flag anything that needs adjustment with a brief note. Then make your edits.

**For high-stakes content:**
For assessments, parent communications, and materials that will go out to many learners, take a little more time. Read through once for accuracy, once for bias, once for CAPS fit. The extra five minutes protects you professionally.

## A note on professional accountability

The SACE Code of Professional Ethics requires educators to act in the best educational interests of their learners, to be honest and accountable, and to maintain professional standards.

AI does not change these obligations — it just adds a new tool to your practice. The Five-Point Responsibility Check exists precisely because the responsibility for what happens in your classroom always remains with you.

You are not just a user of AI. You are a professional who uses AI as one tool among many. That professional identity — your judgment, your relationships, your ethics — is what your learners ultimately rely on.
