## POPIA and AI: what every SA teacher needs to know

The Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) — South Africa's data protection law, which came into full effect in July 2021 — applies to how you handle any personal information in your professional life as an educator. This includes how you use AI tools.

The core question is simple: **could the information you type into Gemini or NotebookLM be used to identify a specific learner, parent, staff member, or colleague?**

If yes, do not type it.

---

## What is "personal information" under POPIA?

POPIA defines personal information broadly. In a school context, it includes:

- **Learner identifiers:** full names, ID numbers, school EMIS numbers, photos
- **Family information:** parent/guardian names, contact numbers, addresses
- **Health and wellbeing data:** medical conditions, disabilities, counselling records
- **Academic records:** marks, assessment results, learning support records
- **Disciplinary records:** incidents, suspensions, hearing outcomes
- **Financial information:** fee payment status, bursary details

<KeyPoint type="important">
POPIA applies regardless of where the data goes. Entering a learner's name and test scores into Gemini is a sharing of personal information under POPIA — even if you are not "publishing" it anywhere publicly.
</KeyPoint>

---

## What happens to data you enter into AI tools?

When you type a message to Gemini:
- Google receives your text and processes it to generate a response
- Depending on your account settings, Google may use your conversations to improve its AI models
- Your data is processed under Google's privacy policy and terms of service

This does not mean Gemini is unsafe. It means you need to treat it like any other cloud tool — with the understanding that what you type leaves your device.

---

## The POPIA-safe rule for AI

**Anonymise before you type.**

If you want AI to help with something involving a learner, replace all identifying details with generic labels before you type.

| Instead of this... | Type this... |
|---|---|
| "Help me write a note about Sipho Dlamini who missed 12 days of school" | "Help me write a note about a Grade 7 learner who missed 12 days of school" |
| "Create a differentiated worksheet for Amahle — she has dyslexia" | "Create a differentiated worksheet for a learner with reading difficulties" |
| "Write a letter to Mrs Nkosi about her son's behaviour in class 8B" | "Write a letter to a parent about a learner's disruptive classroom behaviour" |
| "Grade 10 learner Tshepo scored 23% on his Maths test" | "A Grade 10 learner is performing significantly below grade level in Maths" |

The AI produces the same quality output — it does not need to know the learner's real name.

## What about school information?

The same care applies to school-level sensitive information:

- Do not enter staff salaries, HR records, or disciplinary files into AI tools
- Do not share confidential SGB discussions or financial records
- Do not upload internal school policy documents that are not intended for public viewing
- Treat anything marked "confidential" by your school as off-limits for AI tools

---

## Practical guidance for your school

If your school wants to develop a formal AI usage policy, the key areas to cover are:

1. What categories of information are never shared with AI tools
2. Who is responsible for checking POPIA compliance when using AI
3. How AI-generated content must be reviewed before it reaches learners or parents
4. How to report an accidental data breach (e.g., if you realise you entered personal information)

The South African Department of Education has published guidance on learner data protection — your district or provincial office can direct you to the relevant documents.

<SAExample title="Simple, safe practice">
Ms Sithole, a Head of Department in KwaZulu-Natal, set a simple rule for her department when AI tools became part of their workflow:

"Before you type anything into Gemini, ask yourself: would you be comfortable if the school principal could see exactly what you typed? If yes, type it. If not, remove the personal details first."

She says the rule took two minutes to explain and has prevented any POPIA concerns in her department since.
</SAExample>
