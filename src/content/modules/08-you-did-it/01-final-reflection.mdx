## You made it. Take a moment.

This is the second-to-last lesson in the course. Before you get your certificate, there is one final, important step: reflection.

Not a test. Not a performance. A genuine moment to look back at what you have learned and decide what you are actually going to do with it.

The teachers who get the most out of professional development are not the ones who know the most at the end of the course. They are the ones who turn learning into habit.

---

## What you have covered

Take a moment to think through each module:

**Module 1 — Hello, AI!**
You demystified AI. You understand that it is a tool — not a replacement — and that you, the teacher, are still the expert.

**Module 2 — The Art of the Ask**
You learned the PARTS framework: Persona, Aim, Recipients, Theme, Structure. You know how to write a prompt that gets useful results.

**Module 3 — Save Your Sundays**
You discovered how AI can help with lesson planning, assessments, parent communication, and differentiation — the tasks that eat into your evenings and weekends.

**Module 4 — Bring Your Lessons Alive**
You explored how AI can help you create engaging starters, culturally relevant analogies, deep research, and meaningful special events.

**Module 5 — AI in Your Pocket**
You learned how to use AI on your phone, plan around load shedding, and manage data costs — strategies designed for the South African teaching context.

**Module 6 — Trust But Verify**
You built the critical skills: checking for hallucinations, auditing for bias, protecting privacy under POPIA, navigating academic integrity, and applying the Five-Point Responsibility Check.

**Module 7 — Your Prompt Toolkit**
You know where to find the Prompt Library, the Glossary, and the PARTS Reference Card when you need them.

## A note on imperfect starts

You will not always write the perfect prompt. AI will sometimes give you responses that miss the mark. You will need to rephrase, refine, and try again. This is normal.

The teachers who use AI most effectively are not the ones who got it right immediately. They are the ones who kept trying, kept adjusting, and kept asking: "How can I get more useful output from this tool?"

Your commitment to learning — which this course is evidence of — is the same quality that will make you an effective AI user.
