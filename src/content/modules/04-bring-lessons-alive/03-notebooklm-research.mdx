## NotebookLM: your private research assistant

You met NotebookLM briefly in Module 1. Now you will see what it can really do.

While Gemini draws on general internet knowledge, NotebookLM works only from documents you upload. This makes it unusually useful for teachers — because you can upload your own curriculum documents, textbooks, past papers, or research articles, and then ask questions about them.

Everything NotebookLM tells you is grounded in those sources. It will quote the exact part of the document its answer comes from.

---

## What you can upload to NotebookLM

- CAPS subject guidelines (download these free from the DBE website)
- Approved textbook chapters (as PDF)
- Subject-specific research articles
- Your own lesson notes or summaries
- Past exam papers and memoranda
- DBE subject policy documents
- Any PDF, Google Doc, or copied text you own or have rights to use

<KeyPoint type="important">
Do not upload documents that contain learner personal information (names, ID numbers, assessment records). NotebookLM stores your documents — protect learner privacy in line with POPIA. See Module 6 for detailed guidance.
</KeyPoint>

## How to use NotebookLM for lesson preparation

Here is a practical four-step workflow:

**Step 1 — Upload your source documents**
Go to notebooklm.google.com and create a new notebook. Click "Add source" and upload the relevant PDFs or paste in text. For a Science unit, you might upload the CAPS Life Sciences guidelines and two textbook chapters.

**Step 2 — Ask content questions**
Once your sources are uploaded, ask NotebookLM questions about the content:
- *"What are the key concepts in the Grade 10 photosynthesis section?"*
- *"What does CAPS say about assessment weighting for this topic?"*
- *"Summarise the main differences between meiosis and mitosis from the textbook."*

**Step 3 — Generate structured summaries**
Ask NotebookLM to create structured output from your sources:
- *"Create a one-page summary of the chapter on ecosystems, using bullet points."*
- *"List all the key vocabulary words in this chapter with their definitions."*
- *"What questions does this chapter suggest learners should be able to answer by the end?"*

**Step 4 — Build study guides and question banks**
- *"Generate 10 short-answer questions from this chapter that would be suitable for a Grade 9 test."*
- *"Create a structured study guide for this chapter that learners can use for self-study."*

## Using the Audio Overview feature

NotebookLM has an "Audio Overview" feature that turns your uploaded documents into a podcast-style conversation between two AI voices discussing the content.

This is useful for:
- Reviewing material during your commute
- Sharing a content overview with learners as a revision tool
- Getting a quick sense of a long document without reading it fully

To create one: click "Audio Overview" in your notebook. NotebookLM will generate a 5–15 minute discussion based on your sources.

<SAExample title="CAPS research in 10 minutes">
Mr Hendricks teaches Geography in Cape Town. When he started a new unit on geomorphology, he uploaded the relevant section of the CAPS Geography guidelines and two textbook chapters into NotebookLM.

He asked: *"Based on CAPS, what must Grade 11 learners be able to demonstrate by the end of this unit?"*

NotebookLM pulled out the exact assessment standards with page references. "I used to spend an hour re-reading the CAPS document every time I started a new unit," he says. "Now I get the answer in two minutes — and I can quote the page."
</SAExample>

## Comparing multiple documents

One of NotebookLM's most powerful features: you can upload multiple documents and ask it to compare or synthesise them.

**Try this:** Upload a CAPS document and a textbook chapter for the same topic, then ask:
- *"Does this textbook chapter cover everything required by CAPS? What is missing?"*
- *"Which CAPS assessment standards are addressed in this chapter, and which are not?"*

This kind of cross-document analysis would take a teacher hours to do manually. NotebookLM does it in seconds.

## NotebookLM limitations to know

<Checklist items={[
  "NotebookLM only knows what is in your uploaded documents — it cannot browse the internet",
  "It works best with text-based PDFs, not scanned images (scanned PDFs may not work)",
  "Complex tables and diagrams may not be processed accurately",
  "Always verify answers against the source document before using them in your teaching",
  "Notebooks are stored in your Google account — treat them as you would any sensitive school document"
]} />
