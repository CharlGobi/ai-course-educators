## The first five minutes matter most

The way a lesson begins shapes everything that follows. A strong lesson starter focuses attention, activates prior knowledge, and tells learners: this is going to be worth paying attention to.

Finding fresh, relevant starters for 30 lessons a week is one of those tasks that quietly drains creative energy. AI can give you an almost unlimited supply of ideas — and fast.

---

## What makes a great lesson starter?

Good lesson starters share a few qualities:
- They take 3–7 minutes, no more
- They connect to learners' real lives or the South African context
- They activate existing knowledge without assuming too much
- They create a little curiosity or challenge
- They don't require extra materials or preparation time

AI can generate all of these when you give it the right context.

## Starters that connect to SA life

The most powerful lesson hooks connect unfamiliar content to learners' existing world. AI can help you build those bridges — if you tell it enough about your learners.

<PromptBox example="You are a Grade 8 Physical Sciences teacher in Johannesburg. Design a lesson starter that connects the concept of electrical circuits to something learners experience during load shedding. The activity should: take no more than 5 minutes, require no equipment, make learners think about circuits in a new way, and lead naturally into a formal introduction of series and parallel circuits. Tone: energetic and relevant to South African daily life." />

## Quick starter formats AI handles well

| Format | What to ask for |
|---|---|
| Think-Pair-Share | "Give me a thought-provoking question about [topic] that learners discuss in pairs first, then share with the class." |
| Quick Write | "Give me a one-minute write prompt about [topic] that activates prior knowledge without requiring right answers." |
| Image analysis | "Describe an image I can draw or find that would intrigue learners before a lesson on [topic]." |
| Sorting activity | "Create 8 word cards that learners sort into two groups before I teach [concept]. Include an answer key." |
| Prediction game | "Give me a 'true or false' prediction game with 5 statements about [topic] that challenge common misconceptions." |
| Quote or statistic | "Find a surprising South African statistic or quote that I can use to open a lesson on [topic]." |

## Starter banks for the whole week

One efficient approach: generate a bank of starters on Sunday, then use them throughout the week.

<PromptBox example="You are a Grade 5 Mathematics teacher in South Africa. Generate a bank of 5 mental maths starter activities — one for each school day of the week. Each should take 3 minutes and review number concepts covered this term (fractions, decimals, percentages). Include the activity instructions and the answers. Vary the format each day: Monday — oral questions, Tuesday — whiteboard challenge, Wednesday — estimation, Thursday — number puzzle, Friday — times table game." />
