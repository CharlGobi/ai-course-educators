## Your Sunday afternoons, back

For most teachers, lesson planning happens on Sunday afternoons or late at night after a full week in the classroom. It is necessary work — but it is also the kind of work that eats your personal time relentlessly.

AI cannot plan your lessons for you. It does not know your learners, your school culture, or the particular way your Grade 8s respond to group work. But it can give you a **solid first draft** that you edit in 10 minutes instead of building from scratch in 45.

That difference adds up.

---

## What AI does well for lesson planning

Gemini is particularly good at:

- **Generating a lesson structure** — the bones of a lesson plan with sections for objectives, introduction, main activity, and assessment
- **Suggesting engaging activities** — especially when you give it specific context about your learners
- **Creating differentiated versions** — you can ask for the same lesson at multiple levels
- **Generating resource materials** — worksheets, reading passages, question sets to accompany the lesson
- **Writing from existing material** — paste in a textbook section and ask Gemini to build a lesson from it

---

## The CAPS-alignment caveat

This is important: **Gemini does not automatically know your exact CAPS requirements.**

It knows a lot about South African education in general, and if you ask for "CAPS-aligned" content it will produce something broadly appropriate. But it may not get:

- Exact weighting of formal assessment tasks
- Specific prescribed content lists for your subject and grade
- The precise terminology your CAPS document uses

For lesson planning purposes, AI gives you a great structure. You supply the CAPS accuracy. Always check lesson plans against your actual CAPS document before using them.

<KeyPoint title="The review rule" variant="warning">
Before using any AI-generated lesson plan, check: (1) Are the learning objectives realistic for this grade? (2) Does the timing add up? (3) Are the activities appropriate for your actual class? (4) Does it align with where you are in your CAPS sequence? AI does not know your timetable, your learners, or your term plan.
</KeyPoint>

Here is a complete prompt template you can customise. Copy it, replace the bracketed sections, and paste it into Gemini.

<PromptBox
  title="CAPS lesson plan prompt"
  example="I am a Grade 9 Natural Sciences teacher at a public secondary school in the Western Cape. My class has 35 learners, most of whom speak English as their first additional language.

Create a 60-minute CAPS-aligned lesson plan for Grade 9 Natural Sciences on the topic of chemical equations. This lesson falls in Term 2, Week 4.

Include:
- 2-3 clear learning objectives aligned to the Grade 9 CAPS
- A 10-minute introduction activity that uses a real-world South African context (e.g. cooking, cleaning products, or mining)
- A 35-minute main lesson with teacher explanation and learner activity
- A 10-minute formative assessment (quick quiz or exit activity)
- 5-minute closure/summary

Format as a structured lesson plan with clear headings and timing for each section."
