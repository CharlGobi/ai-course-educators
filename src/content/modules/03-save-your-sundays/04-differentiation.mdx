## Every learner is different. AI can help with that.

Differentiation — adapting your teaching materials to meet the needs of all learners — is one of the most valued and most time-consuming professional skills in teaching.

You might have a class with learners reading at three different levels. You might have a learner who needs extension challenges, another who needs simplified instructions, and a third who learns best through visual examples. Traditionally, creating three versions of the same worksheet would take most of a Sunday afternoon.

With AI, it can take three minutes. Here is what those three versions actually look like — tap each level to see the difference:

## The core differentiation prompt

The key to differentiation with AI is giving it the original material and asking for specific adaptations.

## Simplifying instructions and task sheets

Sometimes learners struggle not with the content, but with the language of the instructions. AI can rewrite task sheets in plain language without changing the learning outcome.

<PromptBox example="Rewrite the following Grade 9 Geography task instructions in simpler language for learners whose home language is not English. ## Extension activities for faster learners

When some learners finish early, you need something meaningful for them to do — not just more of the same.

<PromptBox example="You are a Grade 8 Mathematics teacher. The class has been working on linear equations. Design three extension tasks for learners who finish early. Tasks should require deeper thinking — not just more questions of the same type. Include: one real-world application task, one task that asks learners to create their own equation and explain it, and one puzzle-style challenge. All tasks should be completable in 10–15 minutes without extra resources." />

## Adapting for learners with specific learning needs

AI can assist in adapting materials for learners who have been identified with learning challenges — though always in collaboration with the learner, their parents, and any support professionals at your school.

**For learners with reading difficulties:**
> *"Rewrite this passage using the Flesch-Kincaid Grade 3 reading level. Use short sentences (maximum 10 words), common vocabulary, and add two simple comprehension questions at the end."*

**For learners who are English Language Learners (ELLs):**
> *"Rewrite this task sheet so that a learner whose home language is IsiZulu can understand it without a dictionary. Add a short glossary of the 5 most important subject words at the bottom."*

**For learners who need visual support:**
> *"Convert this written explanation of fractions into a step-by-step visual guide. Describe each step as if explaining what to draw or show. Use simple, visual language."*

<KeyPoint type="warning">
AI does not know your specific learners. Use AI-generated adaptations as a starting point, then adjust for what you know about each learner's strengths and challenges. Avoid sharing learner names or identifying information when working with AI tools (see Module 6 for POPIA guidance).
</KeyPoint>
